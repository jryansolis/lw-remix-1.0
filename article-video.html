The banks have carried the ASX 200 for two years — but with valuations stretched and net interest margins under pressure, are the easy gains behind us? In this episode of Buy Hold Sell, we put seven of the market's most-watched financials to two leading fund managers and ask the only question that matters: buy, hold, or sell?

Below the video, an edited transcript of the calls.

Edited Transcript

Commonwealth Bank (CBA)

Vishal Teckchandani — CBA is trading on a forward P/E north of 28x. Is the most expensive bank in the world a buy, hold or sell?

Steve Johnson Sell
It's a wonderful business at an impossible price. You're paying a tech multiple for low-single-digit earnings growth. I can't make the numbers work.

Carl Capolingua Hold
The chart says don't fight it — the uptrend is intact and dividends keep showing up. I'd hold, but I wouldn't be adding here.

Macquarie Group (MQG)

Steve Johnson Buy
The only one of the majors with genuine global optionality — infrastructure, green energy, asset management. The earnings are lumpy, but the runway is long.

Carl Capolingua Buy
Agreed. It's been basing for six months and just broke out on volume. Momentum and fundamentals finally agree.

GQG Partners (GQG)

Carl Capolingua Buy
A 9% yield, net inflows, and it's trading at a discount to global peers. The market is pricing in key-person risk that I think is overdone.

Steve Johnson Hold
I like it, but fund-flow businesses can turn quickly. I'd want to own it cheaper.
